Website Redesign Case Study

This project focuses on improving the usability, layout, and overall user experience of a website through a structured redesign process.

Problem

The original website had a confusing layout, poor navigation, and lacked a clear visual hierarchy. Users had difficulty finding important information quickly, which made the experience frustrating and inefficient.

Analysis & Critique

After reviewing the original design, several key issues were identified:

  • No clear call-to-action
  • Cluttered layout
  • Inconsistent spacing and alignment
  • Hard-to-read text and poor contrast

Original Design

Design Process

The redesign followed a structured process to improve usability and clarity:

  • Identified user needs and pain points
  • Created basic wireframes for layout improvements
  • Focused on visual hierarchy and readability
  • Simplified navigation and structure

Wireframes / Planning

Testing & Feedback

Feedback was gathered from peers who tested the redesigned layout. Most users found the new design easier to navigate and more visually organized.

Based on feedback, adjustments were made to spacing, button visibility, and overall layout to further improve usability.

Final Result

The final design significantly improves the user experience by making the interface cleaner, more structured, and easier to navigate.
